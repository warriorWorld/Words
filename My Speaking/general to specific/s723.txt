The reading is about rational ignorance. It states that when people purchase some cheap products, they will keep ignorance intentionally, because they don't want to waste their time on minor things.
The professor elaborates on this by giving two examples.
He mentioned he wanted to buy a car, and he compared several cars, all the advantages and disadvantages. But when he went to a store in order to buy some batteries. He just ignored all the information about those batteries, such as which one can last longer, which one has high-quality, and which one costs lower. He just bought the first one that he saw for time's sake.
