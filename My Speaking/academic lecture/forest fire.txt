The lecturer explains how forest fires could benefit animals by giving two examples.
First, she mentioned that forest fires could help animals find food. For instance, when a forest is on fire, turkeys can easily capture insects when insects are trying to escape from trees.
Second, she said that forest fires could benefit young animals find habitats. For example, beetles are a kind of insect who live in certain trees. But these certain trees can secrete a kind of chemical that is harmful to beetles, especially to young beetles. When trees died because of forest fires. Young beetles could live in those dead trees safely.
These are the two benefits that forest fires could offer to animals.
