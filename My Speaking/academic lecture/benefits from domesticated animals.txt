The lecturer explains two benefits from domesticated animals by giving two examples.
First, she mentioned that domesticated animals can provide meat more reliable and more consistently than wild animals. For instance, goats are the first domesticated animal by humans, and goats are very easy to control, and people often bring them from one place to another place without difficulty.
Second, she said that domesticated animals could also provide a variety of foods other than meat. For example, goats could produce milk, and early humans process the milk to yogurt and cheese. In addition, yogurt and cheese can store by early humans extremely simple.
